# Multivariable_Calculus_TUNI

Short weekly summaries from the Multivariable Calculus course.

## Topics Covered

- Parametric curves and vector functions
- Multivariable functions and surfaces
- Riemann sums and integral definition
- Double integrals
- Integration over general regions
- Polar coordinate integrals
- Triple integrals


## Learning Reflection

This repository documents my progress through the Multivariable Calculus course at Tampere University.

### What felt intuitive

- Parametric curves and vector functions were relatively intuitive because they resemble motion in physics (position, velocity, acceleration).
- The idea of volume under a surface using double integrals became clearer once the geometric interpretation was understood.

### What was challenging

- Determining integration limits when the region is not rectangular.
- Understanding why coordinate transformations introduce extra factors (e.g., the `r` term in polar integrals).
- Visualizing triple integration regions in three dimensions.

### Progress Over the Weeks

At the beginning, many concepts felt abstract, especially when interpreting integration regions or coordinate changes.  
However, after working through the exercises and visualizing the problems geometrically, the structure of multivariable integration became clearer.

By the later weeks, changing coordinate systems (polar, cylindrical, spherical) started to feel more natural, especially when the geometry of the region suggested a better coordinate system.

Overall, the course gradually shifted from abstract definitions to geometric intuition and problem-solving strategies.
